Pizza Rolls – Totino’s Copycat

Preptime: 30 minutes     Cook Time: 15 minutes

Total Time: 45 minutes     Yields: 5 to 6

These pizza rolls are a homemade copycat of the ones I grew up eating. My mom always had a batch ready for school lunches or after-school snacks. They’re like a better, fresher version of Totino’s, with piping hot, gooey cheese that oozes out the moment you take a bite.

Ingredients

FILLING

  • 1 1/2 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 3/4 cup pizza sauce
  • 1/2 cup chopped turkey pepperoni
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/4 cup grated parmesan
  • 1 tablespoon chopped basil
  • 1 tablespoon chopped parsley

FOR ASSEMBLY

  • 25 wonton wrappers
  • vegetable oil, for frying

    • Instructions

    1. Make the filling: In a bowl, mix together the shredded mozzarella, pizza sauce, chopped turkey pepperoni, red pepper flakes, oregano, parmesan, basil, and parsley until everything is well combined. Set aside.
    2. Stuff and assemble: Have a small bowl of water ready for sealing. Lay out the wonton wrappers in a diamond shape so the corners point up, down, left, and right.
    3. Spoon a small dollop of filling (about 1 to 1 ½ teaspoons) into the center of each wrapper.
    4. Dip your finger in water and run it along all the edges of the wrapper.
    5. Fold the bottom corner up over the filling to form a triangle.
    6. Fold in the left and right corners toward the center.
    7. Then fold the top corner down and press to seal everything shut like an envelope. Make sure all the edges are tightly sealed so no filling leaks out.
    8. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan to 350°F (175°C). Once hot, carefully add the rolls in batches and fry until golden brown and crispy on all sides, about 2 to 3 minutes. Remove and place on a paper towel–lined plate to drain any excess oil.
    9. Serve hot with extra pizza sauce or ranch for dipping!
